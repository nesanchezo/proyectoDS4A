Is the matched subset from the implemented algorithm representative of the overall dataset?

As there will be an implementation of a predictive model that allows the prediction of the scores the admited students might obtain after developing their studyies in the university, then it is necessary to verify if the subset obtained from birthday matching can still represent the performance of the overall students. Considering that the skills from Saber11 might become input variables for the predictive model, we performed a hypothesis test on every skill evaluated in the SaberPro and Saber11 in order to verify that the subset still carries enough statistical power in representing the overall population.

The skills to compare in both subsets are: `MOD_RAZONA_CUANTITAT_PUNT`, `MOD_LECTURA_CRITICA_PUNT`, `MOD_COMPETEN_CIUDADA_PUNT`, `MOD_INGLES_PUNT`, `MOD_COMUNI_ESCRITA_PUNT`, and `PUNT_GLOBAL` in the SaberPro exam. And `PUNT_SOCIALES_CIUDADANAS`, `PUNT_INGLES`, `PUNT_LECTURA_CRITICA`, `PUNT_MATEMATICAS`, and `PUNT_C_NATURALES` in the Saber11 exam.

We can observe that the t-tests rejects the hypothesis meaning that the means and distributions of every skill in both sets are not significantly different from each other. This can be noticed by establishing a significance threshold of λ=0.05. From this, the skills corresponding to MOD_COMPETEN_CIUDADA_PUNT and MOD_COMUNI_ESCRITA_PUNT in the SaberPro exam, the distributions are different.

On the other hand, this behavior does not occur with any of the skills in the Saber11 exam.

If we take into account that the predictive model will not have as inputs the variables corresponding to the scores obtained by students in the SaberPro exam, as these are in fact the quantities to be predicted, then we can validate that the subset obtained from the algorithm based in matching the birthdates of the students, is valid and can be successfully used as source of information to fit the predictive model.
